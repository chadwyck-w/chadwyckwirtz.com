Interactive Video Wall

I created this LED video wall as an interactive installation in the LG Sunnyvale Lobby
I was given the opportunity to develop an interactive installation in the lobby of the newly remodeled Sunnyvale LG innovation office. I figured the best interaction would immediately make a positive impact, be fun and look great.
We installed a matrix of addressable RGB LED panels covering almost the entire lobby entry wall, nearly 8 feet by 14 feet in size. The matrix was connected to an HDMI converter box in a server room on the other side of the box and then to a Mac Pro running the visualization software. The Mac Pro used a webcam as its input and tracked the users face to drive the interactive visualization.
Two prototype interactions were created. The first was an interactive geometric visualization that you could control with your body. The second tracked your face and magnified it to fill the entire wall. Most people preferred the second one as it was more personal and less abstract.
