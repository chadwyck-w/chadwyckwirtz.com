Nokia Steel HR

Animation prototyping kit
The Steel HR is a comprehensive activity tracker that automatically recognizes when you walk, run, swim, and sleep. Additionally, more than 10 activities can be recognized—from ping-pong to volleyball to dancing.
The discreet digital notifications screen embedded in the analog watchface makes sure you’re always in the know, wherever you go.
The Nokia design team was looking for ways to improve the user the experience of the existing Steel HR hardware. Many users found the time and notifications displayed the tiny OLED display difficult to read. Some users also believed that the device had the potential to show more interesting information using animation.
The two designers working on the watch realized that animations that they created on their computer screens would look different on the device due to the display technology.
With this problem in mind, I suggested that I could create a tool that allowed a designer to create an animation in Adobe After Effects and play it on the device screen.
I created a Mac application that captured a portion of the designers screen and streamed it to a 128x96 pixel OLED using I2C protocol over a MPSSE USB dongle. The OLED very closely matched the specifications of the watch screen and was enclosed behind a mask representative of the watch face.
While it could be tricky to install the drivers for the MPSSE device, the designers I shared the kit with found it to be useful for quickly prototyping new animations.
In addition to this application I created a device to simulate the Withings Smart Scale display.
