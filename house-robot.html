Claire's Robot

How tough could it be to design and build a robot with a seven year old?

Design Brief

This story starts with a drawing that my daughter created of a little robot that she received as a gift for Christmas. That drawing turned into a conversation on a long car ride home. We talked about how she imagined interacting with her little robot and the things that it could do. Along the way I suggested that maybe if we were clever we could design and create our own house robot. We discussed various characteristics that it could have, what it might look like and what it could do.
I wanted to build a robot, self contained and mobile, with voice interaction and computer vision. Claire wanted a robot that has a vacuum and an arm.

Our Robot Goals

  • Has an arm
  • Has a vacuum
  • Can Listen
  • Can Speak
  • Can See
  • Can Move

Construction

Simple and made from materials available from the hardware store. Constructed from PVC pipe, lexan and aluminum stock.
The Chassis
The chassis is a simple tank drive / caster setup using two optically encoded motors from a first generation Roomba.
Head Bearing
The robot will have a head unit that can rotate roughly 90 degrees independently of the robots body
Distance Sensor
The robot will use a simple ultrasonic distance sensor to detect obstacles. You can see the PVC cap that will become its head unit sitting on the right.
Completed Chassis
Here is the completed chassis sitting on the workspace.
Arduino
The Arduino is used to control the motors via a simple motor controller board and read the motor encoders. It is also hooked up to the ping (utrasonic) sensor.
Assembled Body

Development

Motor controller
I created a simple loop for the Arduino that reads commands from the serial port and detects changes in the optical sensors via an interrupt
Eyes
Detect a face using OpenCV and react to it
Ears
Snowboy, a hotword detection engine to listen for the robots name and forward the audio to the Google voice to text service. The robot will take and action if the response matches any of its commands.
Mouth
Espeak TTS for the robots voice. eSpeak is a compact open source software speech synthesizer for English and other languages.
Face
Using luma.oled to create a "happy" face for the robot.
